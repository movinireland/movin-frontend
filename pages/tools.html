🎉 First-time buyers in Ireland have major advantages

As a first-time buyer in Ireland you benefit from higher borrowing limits, government schemes, and tax rebates that can save you tens of thousands of euro.

HTB applies to new builds only. The refund is the lower of: €30,000, 10% of the purchase price, or the amount of income tax and DIRT paid in the previous 4 years. Property must be ≤ €500,000. Revenue.ie has full eligibility details.
First Home Scheme

Max price (Dublin) €500,000
Max price (other areas) €450,000
Max equity share 30%
Applies to New builds only
How it works

The government and participating banks take an equity share in your home to help you bridge the gap between what you can borrow/save and the purchase price. You buy them out over time.

First-time buyer borrowing limits
Central Bank rules allow first-time buyers to borrow more
Loan-to-Income (LTI)
First-time buyers can borrow up to 4× their gross income (vs 3.5× for second-time buyers). On a combined income of €100,000 that's €400,000 vs €350,000.
FTB advantage
Loan-to-Value (LTV)
First-time buyers need a minimum 10% deposit. Second and subsequent buyers need 20%. On a €350,000 home that's €35,000 vs €70,000.
FTB advantage
Local Authority Home Loan
Government-backed mortgage at fixed rates of 2.495%–2.745% for eligible first-time buyers who can't get a mortgage from banks. Available through your local council.
