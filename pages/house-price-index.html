Where the data comes from

The figures shown here are derived from the CSO Residential Property Price Index (RPPI) — the official measure of changes in the price of residential properties purchased in Ireland.

The RPPI is built from stamp-duty filings sent by Revenue, so it covers actual completed sales rather than asking prices. It's the most reliable indicator of what Irish homes are really selling for, broken down nationally and by region.

How to read the numbers

  • Index value: the price level relative to January 2005 (= 100). An index of 165 means prices are 65% above 2005 levels.
  • YoY change: annual percentage change, helpful for trend-spotting.
  • Average price: the typical sale price across all property types in the region.
  • Caveats: averages hide huge variation by Eircode — a Dublin 4 home and a Dublin 22 home are both \"Dublin\". Use these as a starting point, not the final word.

How to use it

Buyers: compare the trend in the area you're shopping. If prices rose 5% last year and your offer is at last year's asking price, you may need to come up.

Sellers: use the trend to inform your asking price. Pair it with sold prices for properties on your street — see our sold prices page.

Investors: look at 5-year change to gauge longer momentum, and at rental data for yield.
